About Me

The person
behind the
passion.

I believe we were all made in the image of a creative God and that art is one of the most beautiful ways we reflect that.

01

Where it all began

My love for art began in childhood with simple sketches that grew into something much more meaningful. I became known for my handmade birthday and holiday cards, which I called "Charmark" cards. They weren't just cards; they were an early signal that creating for others was something I was made to do.

I was such a perfectionist as a kid that my teachers once took away my eraser privileges because I'd spend entire class periods reworking the same drawing. Painting changed that. With acrylics, and later oils, I learned to work with mistakes rather than against them.

02

Finding my medium

In high school, I discovered acrylics and fell in love — then oils gave me an even deeper freedom to experiment. Junior year I took AP Art, started doing commissions for close friends and family, and realized that sharing art with people I love is one of the greatest privileges I know.

Since then I've been blessed to reach more and more people, which is what led me to creating this website. Every piece I make carries the prayer that it brings genuine joy to whoever receives it.

03

Faith at the center

My faith is at the heart of everything I create. I write Bible verses on the backs of my paintings. I thank God before I pick up a brush. I was saved two years ago and encountered the perfect love of Jesus — and that changed how I see my gifts entirely.

"Each of you should use whatever gift you have received to serve others, as faithful stewards of God's grace in its various forms."

1 Peter 4:10

I believe that we are all made in His image — and that we were created to create. Every human has a gift unique to them. I think that’s so beautiful! What’s your gift? Now, how can you use that for good?

04

Life beyond the studio

I'm currently a student at Berry College in Rome, GA, double majoring in Communication and Sport Leadership & Strategy. I manage the women's basketball team, ref and play intramural sports, and serve as a Lettie Pate Whitehead Foundation scholar.

Art and athletics might sound like opposites, but to me they're both about discipline, creativity under pressure, and showing up for others.
